[color=#ff2fff][head=2]Soap[/head]
══════════════[/color]
Here's a simple soap recipe to start you out in the wonderful world of chemistry, made from simple station materials you can find just about everywhere.

[color=green][italic]// Tools: beaker.
// Machines: Grinder, Microwave or Hotplate
// Reagents: Paper. 2 plastic. 1 salt packet.[/italic][/color]
[mono][bold][color=red]): Soap[/color][/bold]
 + [color=purple]1[/color] ash
 + [color=purple]2[/color] plastic
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Grind[/bold][/color]          [color=#f39f27].= ~10u Oil[/color]
 + [color=purple]10[/color] Water
 + [color=purple]1 [/color] salt packet
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Heat[/bold][/color]
 [color=red]∴ [bold]Soap[/bold][/color][/mono]

Did your eyes glaze over looking at that? No worries, I'm one step ahead with an easier guide.

 ▪ Ash is the first step. Luckily this is very easy. Grab some
   paper and light it on fire! It burns pretty, too.
 ▪ Grab two pieces of plastic. Stuff it and the ash in a grinder
   (better known as chef's spiny machine).
 ▪ Grind it up! Make sure you don't forget the beaker.
   The machine won't work without the beaker.
 ▪ Alternatively, ask the chef to do it for you! They have
   nothing important to do, anyway.
 ▪ Add a splash ([bold]not[/bold] a dash) of water to the beaker.
 ▪ Mix in a packet of salt.
 ▪ Lastly, pop it in the microwave for just over a minute.
 ▪ [bold]Kapow![/bold] A brand new soap is made!

[color=#ff2fff][head=2]Meat Soap[/head]
══════════════[/color]
You can never have enough soap, and good news for you! All you need for this soap is an expedition into the kitchen. Make sure chef doesn't catch you stealing their meat!

[color=green][italic]// Tools: beaker.
// Machines: Grinder, Microwave or Hotplate
// Reagents: Paper. raw meat. 1 salt packet.[/italic][/color]
[mono][bold][color=red]): Soap[/color][/bold]
 + [color=purple]1[/color] ash
 + [color=purple]2[/color] raw meat
[bold][color=red]*OR*[/color][/bold]
 + [color=purple]3[/color] rotten meat
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Grind[/bold][/color]          [color=#f39f27].= ~10u Fat[/color]
 + [color=purple]10[/color] Water
 + [color=purple]10[/color] Blood
 + [color=purple]1 [/color] salt packet
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Heat[/bold][/color]
 [color=red]∴ [bold]Soap, Cooked Protein[/bold][/color][/mono]

Be honest, you didn't even look at that this time. But that's okay, because I'm going to repeat it in normal words again.

 ▪ Burn some paper! Or find ash somewhere else.
 ▪ Next you'll need 2 slabs of meat, any meat will do. If you
   can't find the fresh stuff, 3 slabs of rotten meat works, too.
 ▪ Grinding time!
 ▪ Add a splash ([bold]NOT[/bold] a dash) of water to the beaker.
 ▪ Now, a splash of blood. Make sure it's the red kind of blood.
 ▪ A dash of salt.
 ▪ A minute in the microwave to top it off.
 ▪ [bold]Kapow![/bold] Your soap is made. But even better?
 ▪ Your now boiling hot chemical mixture has cooked the
   leftover protein. You're left with a "delicious" soap soup!

[color=#ff2fff][head=2]Space Lube[/head]
══════════════[/color]
This one is tough. You'll need cooperation from both the chef [bold]and[/bold] the chemist. Three jugs of stuff from the chemist, and chef's microwave. After that, it's easy cooking.

[color=green][italic]// Tools: large beaker.
// Machines: Microwave or Hotplate
// Reagents: iron, silicon, oxygen. OR crate D, crate P.[/italic][/color]
[mono][bold][color=red]): Space Lube[/color][/bold]
 + [color=purple]1[/color] iron
 + [color=purple]1[/color] silicon
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Heat[/bold][/color]              [color=#777777]= 2 Fersilicite[/color]
 + [color=purple]2[/color] oxygen
 + [color=purple]2[/color] Water
 [color=red]∴ [bold]10u Lube[/bold][/color][/mono]

This one is real simple. Put a bunch of stuff in a beaker and heat it up. The labels are on the jugs.

 ▪ From chemist you'll need: iron, silicon, and oxygen.
 ▪ Add equal amounts iron and silicon. Add too much and
   you'll have problems later.
 ▪ Heat up the beaker. There's lots of ways to do this.
 ▪ Figure out how much liquid you have. Remember it.
 ▪ Remember how much liquid you have? You need exactly
   that amount in both oxygen and Water.
 ▪ [bold]Kapow![/bold] Your Lube is ready to go! Be careful of spills.
                                                      [color=green]// [italic]Science Fact:[/italic][/color]
[color=#ff2fff][head=2]Space Glue[/head]                          [color=green]// [italic]You've just disproved[/italic][/color]
══════════════[/color]                 [color=green]// [italic]conservation of matter theory![/italic][/color]
Space glue isn't difficult to produce. All you need is Lube and Slime. Please source your Slime ethically.

[color=green][italic]// Tools: beaker.
// Machines: Microwave or Hotplate
// Reagents: Space Lube, Slime.[/italic][/color]
[mono][bold][color=red]): space glue[/color][/bold]
 + [color=purple]1[/color] Space Lube
 + [color=purple]1[/color] Slime
[color=blue]▶ [bold]Heat[/bold][/color]
 [color=red]∴ [bold]2u space glue[/bold][/color][/mono]

If you've been following along there isn't much that needs explaining here. Just mix and heat!

 ▪ Get yourself some Space Lube. Luckily I've already shown
   you how right up there! ↑↑↑
 ▪ Get yourself some Slime. You can grind up roaches,
   slimeballs, or borrow the "blood" of your gelatinous
   coworkers. Just ask for their permission first.
 ▪ Combine equal parts Lube and Slime.
 ▪ [bold]Kapow![/bold] Heat it up and you're good to slow!

[color=#ff2fff][head=2]Getting Prepared[/head]
══════════════[/color]
We're getting into some hard recipes, with high costs. You'll need machines and bananas from here on out. Be prepared to prepare a workspace.

 ▪ Get yourself as many bananas as you can manage.
 ▪ You'll need a grinder. Better remembered as the machine
   chef has that grinds things.
 ▪ You also need a centrifuge. It's a spining machine chemist
   owns. Chemist might even give you theirs if they're nice.
 ▪ If you're having trouble with the machines, call for backup!
   Scientists and engineers love helping.
 ▪ A vial is a pivotal item: You need it for the centrifuge. If
   chemist is nice they can give you this, too. If they're not
   nice, you can print it in an autolathe or buy it from cargo.
