Computers. Machines. Airlocks. It's nearly impossible to exist these days without them. But less common is the knowledge of how to build and maintain these devices. Not to worry! In this [bold]beginner's guide[/bold], I'll teach you everything you need to start building your very own space station.

[color=#f39f27][head=2]Tools of the Trade[/head]
══════════════[/color]
The first step is assembling your tools. There's countless tools for countless purposes, but here's the 8 most basic ones we'll be using.

 ▪ [bold]Wrench[/bold]          ▫  Anchoring
 ▪ [bold]Screwdriver[/bold]    ▫  Screwing
 ▪ [bold]Crowbar[/bold]          ▫  Prying
 ▪ [bold]Wirecutter[/bold]       ▫  Snipping
 ▪ [bold]Welding tool[/bold]    ▫  Welding
 ▪ [bold]Welding mask[/bold]  ▫  Welding
 ▪ [bold]Multitool[/bold]           ▫  Pulsing
 ▪ [bold]LV cable[/bold]

With the exception of a welding mask and a multitool, these are all dispensed from a [bold]YouTool[/bold] vending machine found in most NT facilities. All of these tools -- including a welding mask -- can also be made in an autolathe.

We're not done yet! You'll need a utility belt to hold all this, unless you like carrying piles of miscellaneous metal and plastic. Utility belts can also be crafted in an autolathe with a bit of cloth and steel.

[color=#f39f27][head=2]Basic Construction[/head]
══════════════[/color]
Within each of ourselves is a vast repository of knowledge; You just need to learn how to get in the right headspace.

Close your eyes. Picture a hammer. Perhaps the letter "[bold]G[/bold]"? You'll be greeted by an overwhelming sea of ideas. Focus your search to one specific idea, and once you've grasped it, imagine a "ghost" of that idea in the space in front of you.

Now start building! Confusing I know, but trust me when I say that everyone uses this "ghost" strategy.

[color=blue]▼ Anchor[/color]          [color=green]▲ Anchor[/color]

You'll find that many constructions are nearly identical. Above we can see that a firelock and a glass firelock differ simply in that one has glass (shocking!).

It's therefore trivial to swap between the two. Anchor, pry, and anchor again for some free glass! But be careful, constructions are picky about where they can be built. Engineers posses a tool to circumvent some restrictions (the RCD), and some facilities make use of this workaround during construction.

The second step in de-constructing an airlock will require a precise use of your wirecutters. You need to cut every wire in the airlock, and avoid bolting the door. This is covered in more detail in the next section.

[color=#f39f27][head=2]Hacking an Airlock[/head]
══════════════[/color]
To deconstruct an airlock we need to learn how to hack one. For this we need [bold]insulated gloves[/bold]. These are standard uniform for engineer, and that's where I'll leave it.

More often than not the objective of hacking is to cut power. This is why we need insulation; We'll be cutting live wires. Once the power wires are cut, the airlock powers down, and the rest of the wires are harmless.

The most likely issue you'll have is the [bold]bolting wire[/bold]. When cut, the airlock bolts. We can't continue disassembly if the door is bolted, so how do we fix it? The [bold]multitool[/bold] comes to our rescue. It's able to give a tiny shock to the wire connection, and this results in different behavior for each wire.

If we need to undo a bolting, repair the bolting wire with your wirecutters and pulse. [bold]Kapow![/bold] The door unbolts! Now we can carry on avoiding that wire until the power has been severed.

There's several other wires in an airlock, but to be concise:
 ▪ [bold]TIMR[/bold]: Sets how fast the airlock closes.
 ▪ [bold]SAFE[/bold]: Prevents the airlock from crushing people.
 ▪ [bold]LOG[/bold]: Manages the airlock's usage history.
 ▪ [bold]BLIT[/bold]: Controls displaying the bolt light.
 ▪ [bold]AIA[/bold]: Gives access to overseer AIs.

A final tip for the engineers: wire layouts are the same for each access group! Once you've learned one, you've learned the whole department.
